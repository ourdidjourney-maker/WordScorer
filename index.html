Word Scorer — How It Works
What is Word Scorer?

Word Scorer is a user-defined emotional mechanics engine. You assign weights to words. The engine measures how those words behave across your text — their mass, velocity, density, pacing, and distribution. It doesn't tell you what your text means. It shows you what your text does.

Core philosophy: "My weights aren't your weights." The dictionary is yours. The tool just measures.

The Dictionary

Every word has two value columns: − negative and + positive. Fill one or both.

If only the positive value is filled, the word scores normally. If only the negative value is filled, it subtracts from the score (when polarity is on). If both are filled, the word becomes a dual word — enabling the Duality Score panel to show two parallel readings of the text.

Words sort alphabetically when you leave a row. Import and export your dictionary as JSON to save and share your emotional fingerprint.

Velocity & Chains

When consecutive words both score at or above the velocity threshold, they form a chain. Instead of adding their values, the engine multiplies them exponentially using the chain multiplier.

At multiplier ×2.0: a 2-word chain scores ×2, a 3-word chain scores ×4, a 4-word chain scores ×8. The velocity bonus is the extra points added on top of the base score. Toggle velocity off to see pure base weight only.

Toggles

Velocity on/off — disables chain bonuses. Total score becomes pure base weight.
Polarity on/off — when off, all negative values are treated as positive. Measures emotional intensity regardless of direction.
Caps scoring — when on, ALL CAPS dictionary words score 1.5× their base value.
Highlights on/off — toggles colour highlights in the text area.

Metrics Reference
Intensity Map
Heatmap of score density across the text. Orange = high concentration. Green = low.
Velocity Ratio
% of total score from chain bonuses. High = emotion is feeding itself. Low = words are placed deliberately.
Pronoun Tracker
I/me vs you/your vs we/our counts. Zero I/me + high you + high we = recruitment or sermon pattern.
Tone vs Mass
Gap between calm tone and heavy payload. High = controlled rhetoric delivered in measured language.
Draft vs Rant
Was this constructed deliberately or vented emotionally? Based on velocity ratio and repetition density.
Authorship Fingerprint
Sermon · Rant · Persuasion · Essay · Neutral — synthesised from all structural signals.
Spike Distance (SDI)
Gap between emotional spikes. Surgical = isolated precision. Massed = clustered avalanche. Distributed = controlled even spread.
Text Weight
Raw base mass with no bonuses. Shows which words carry the most weight and their % of total.
Polarity Composite
Positive vs negative weight split. Tension = how hard the text pulls in both directions simultaneously.
Duality Score
For dual words: Score A (negative reading) vs Score B (positive reading). Range = interpretive ambiguity.
Absolutes Counter
Certainty words — none, never, always, every, etc. High count = closed worldview, no nuance available.
Hedge Density
Uncertainty words — maybe, perhaps, might, seems, etc. High hedging in high-mass text = anxious or deliberately vague.
Imperative Counter
Command-form sentences. High imperatives in calm drafted text = orders disguised as comfort.
Sentence Openers
What word starts each sentence. I/me = self-focused. You = reader-targeting. We/our = collectivising.
Char Density
Total character count shown in the top bar — with spaces and without. Avg word length shown as a subtitle. Low avg = punchy/simple. High avg = complex/academic.
Character Breakdown
Full character analysis — with spaces, no spaces, letters only, symbols only. Symbols = punctuation, emoticons, special characters. Shows how much of the text is pure language vs emphasis and decoration.
Tips

Export stats — click "↓ export stats" below the badge strip to download a full JSON snapshot of all metrics for auditing or comparison.

Import a dictionary — load someone else's dictionary to score text through their emotional lens. This is the empathy engine — inhabiting another person's native emotional tone.

The teaching challenge — write something emotionally resonant using only low-weight words and short sentences. Low score, high feeling. That's the craft.
